India's digital journey over the past decade has been nothing short of extraordinary. From UPI revolutionizing payments to Aadhaar creating a universal identity system, the country has demonstrated that large-scale digital transformation is possible in a developing economy.

But as we stand at this crossroads, critical questions demand our attention.

## The Access Gap

While urban India enjoys world-class digital services, rural connectivity remains patchy. The digital divide isn't just about internet access — it's about meaningful participation in the digital economy.

## Data Sovereignty

As Indian data flows through global platforms, the question of who controls and benefits from this data becomes increasingly important. Our regulatory framework must evolve to protect citizen data while enabling innovation.

## The AI Question

With artificial intelligence reshaping every sector, India must invest in building indigenous AI capabilities rather than becoming solely dependent on foreign technology.

## The Path Forward

The next decade of Digital India must focus on three pillars:

1. **Universal meaningful connectivity** — not just access, but quality access
2. **Digital literacy** — ensuring every citizen can participate safely
3. **Indigenous technology development** — building, not just consuming

The promise of technology is inclusion. Let us ensure India fulfills that promise.
