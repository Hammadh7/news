The Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology has released a draft framework for artificial intelligence regulation in India, marking a significant step in the country's approach to governing emerging technologies.

## Key Provisions

The framework outlines several critical areas:

### Transparency Requirements
AI systems used in critical sectors like healthcare, finance, and governance will need to meet strict transparency standards, including explainability requirements.

### Data Protection
Building on existing data protection laws, the framework introduces specific provisions for AI training data, including consent requirements and bias auditing.

### Innovation Sandbox
A regulatory sandbox will allow startups and researchers to test AI applications in a controlled environment before full deployment.

## Industry Response

The tech industry has largely welcomed the framework, calling it a balanced approach that doesn't stifle innovation while addressing legitimate concerns about AI safety.

## Global Context

India's framework joins a growing list of national AI regulations worldwide, positioning the country as a thoughtful leader in technology governance among developing nations.

The draft is open for public consultation for the next 60 days.
