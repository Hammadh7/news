The India Meteorological Department (IMD) has released its first long-range forecast for the 2026 Southwest Monsoon, predicting above-normal rainfall that is expected to boost agricultural output across the country.

## Forecast Details

The IMD has predicted that the monsoon season (June-September) will bring rainfall at 104% of the Long Period Average (LPA), categorizing it as "above normal."

## Regional Distribution

- **Northwest India**: Expected to receive above-normal rainfall
- **Central India**: Normal to above-normal precipitation predicted
- **South Peninsula**: Good rainfall expected, particularly in Karnataka and Kerala
- **Northeast India**: Normal monsoon activity anticipated

## Impact on Agriculture

Agricultural experts say the forecast is excellent news for the kharif season, with farmers expected to increase sowing area for rice, pulses, and oilseeds.

## Water Reservoir Levels

With the positive monsoon outlook, water resource managers are optimistic about reservoir replenishment, which would ease water supply concerns in several states.

The detailed forecast with state-wise predictions will be released in May.
