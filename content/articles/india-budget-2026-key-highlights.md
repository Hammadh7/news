The Union Budget 2026-27, presented by the Finance Minister in Parliament today, has introduced several landmark measures that promise to reshape India's economic landscape.

## Tax Reforms

The most anticipated announcement was the revision of income tax slabs under the new regime. The tax-free income limit has been raised, providing relief to millions of middle-class taxpayers across the country.

## Infrastructure Push

A record allocation has been made for infrastructure development, with a focus on:

- **Railways**: New high-speed rail corridors connecting tier-2 cities
- **Highways**: Completion targets for the Bharatmala project
- **Digital Infrastructure**: Expansion of 5G networks to rural areas

## Social Welfare

The budget introduces new schemes for:

- Affordable housing in urban areas
- Skill development programs for youth
- Enhanced healthcare coverage under Ayushman Bharat

## Market Reaction

The stock markets responded positively to the budget announcements, with the Sensex rallying over 500 points in early trading. Analysts have termed the budget as "growth-oriented" while maintaining fiscal discipline.

## Expert Analysis

Leading economists have praised the balanced approach of the budget, noting its focus on both short-term relief and long-term structural reforms. The emphasis on green energy and sustainable development has been particularly well-received.

The opposition, however, has raised concerns about the fiscal deficit targets and the allocation for rural employment schemes.
