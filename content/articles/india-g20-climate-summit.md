India has taken center stage at the G20 Summit with a bold proposal for a new Green Development Fund that would help developing nations transition to clean energy without sacrificing economic growth.

## The Proposal

The Green Development Fund, as proposed by India, would:

- Pool resources from developed nations to support clean energy transitions
- Provide technology transfer mechanisms for renewable energy
- Create a framework for carbon credit trading that benefits developing economies
- Establish a climate resilience fund for vulnerable nations

## Global Response

The proposal has received strong support from the Global South, with several African and Asian nations expressing solidarity with India's position.

## India's Own Commitments

India also announced accelerated timelines for its renewable energy targets, demonstrating leadership by example in the climate action space.

## Expert Analysis

Climate policy experts have called the proposal "pragmatic and ambitious," noting that it addresses the long-standing demand for climate justice while proposing concrete mechanisms for implementation.

The summit continues tomorrow with negotiations on the final communique.
