The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) today announced the detailed mission architecture and timeline for Chandrayaan-4, India's most ambitious lunar mission to date.

## Mission Objectives

Chandrayaan-4 aims to achieve what only three nations have accomplished before — bringing back samples from the Moon's surface. The mission will target the lunar south pole region, building on the success of Chandrayaan-3's historic landing.

## Technical Details

The mission will involve multiple spacecraft components:

- An orbiter module for lunar orbit insertion
- A lander designed for precise south pole touchdown
- A sample collection and ascent module
- A return capsule for Earth re-entry

## Timeline

ISRO Chairman confirmed that the mission is on track for a launch window in early 2028, with sample return expected within 30 days of landing.

## International Collaboration

The mission includes collaborative instruments from partner space agencies, marking a new era of international cooperation in India's space program.

This announcement cements India's position as a major player in the global space exploration community.
